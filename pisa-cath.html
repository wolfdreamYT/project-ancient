Cathedral of Pisa

Published on April 12, 2026

The Cathedral of Pisa is located in the same square as the leaning tower of pisa, and was built to demonstrate the power and wealth of Pisa during the medieval period. The cathedral is known for the bronze door, which the legend says if you stare at it for 5 minutes then good luck will come to you in a form of love.

The cathedral is also known for its columns, golden mosaics, and the golden ceiling. Today the cathedral is known as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, ensuring the preservation of the famous attraction.

Data

  • Location: Pisa, Italy
  • Built: 1063–1092
  • Architectural Style: Romanesque
  • Part of: Piazza dei Miracoli
  • Status: Active cathedral
