Statue of David

Published on April 1, 2026

The Statue of David is a sculpture by Michelangelo and is about the famous story of david and goliath. Michelangelo started working on it in 1501, and finished by 1504 and featured details such as veins and muscles, which fascinates people to this day.

The Statue of David was first placed outside the Palazzo Vecchio in Florence’s Piazza della Signoria. In 1873, they moved the original statue to the Galleria dell’Accademia in Florence because they were afraid of accidental damage. A replica was made to replace the statue in the Piazza, and if you look closely and compare the 2 statues, you can see the difference.

The statue is a whopping 5.17 meters tall, and Michelangelo intentionally designed the statue to be a specific size because it was originally meant to be viewed from street level. In 1991, a man attacked the statue with a hammer and chipped off the toes, and Florence had to glue them back on. This increased security a lot.
