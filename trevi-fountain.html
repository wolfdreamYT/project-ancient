The Trevi Fountain

Published on March 31, 2026

The Trevi Fountain was built and completed in 1762 during the baroque period, and was designed by Nicola Salvi, and finished by Giuseppe Pannini. In 1730s, the Pope Clement XII had decided the old fountain needed an upgrade, so he started a design contest which Nicola Salvi won.

Nicola Salvi started working on the Trevi Fountain in 1732, but because his project was so big, he died in 1751 before he finished it. Giuseppe Pannini decided to step in and finish the fountain, and after about 30 years, the Trevi Fountain was completed.

To this day, the Trevi Fountain is one of the most famous fountains in the world, and people toss coins in it to ensure they return to Rome one day. In reality, the money goes to the Caritas, a Catholic charity for people in need.

Data

  • Built in: 1732-1762
  • Built By: Giuseppe Pannini
  • Designed by: Nicola Salvi
  • Commissioned by: Pope Clement XII
  • Width: 161.3 feet
  • Height: 86 feet
