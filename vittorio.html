Vittorio Emanuele II Monument

Published on April 1, 2026

The Vittorio Emanuele II Monument is a famous monument and was built to honor Victor Emmanuel II, the first king of unified italy. The monument features a museum and the famous Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.

The Tomb of the Unknwon soldier is constantly guarded by guards 24/7, with an eternal flame to honor those who went to war and never came back and who were forgotton.

When building the Vittorio Emanuele II Monument, they had to demolish old medieval and ancient buildings on capitoline hill to be able to build the full monument. The famous bronze Statue of Victor Emmanuel II on the horse is one of the largest equestrian statues in the world, making 50 years of construction worth it.

Data

  • Built in: 1885-1935
  • Built By: Multiple Architects
  • Designed by: Giuseppe Sacconi
  • Honored to: Victor Emmanuel II
  • Width: 443 feet
  • Height: 230 feet
