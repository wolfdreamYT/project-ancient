St. Mark's Basilica

Published on April 12, 2026

The Saint Marks Basilica is one of the most famous churchs in Italy known for the stunning architecture and design. The Basilica is located in the Saint Marks Square, another popular tourist attraction known the designs as well.

The basilica was originally built during the 11th century and was used to house the relics of saint mark which were brought to venice from alexandria. Eventually, the interior was decorated with mosaics, marble, gold, and more.

Inside the basilica you can see the original horses that were once displayed out front. Today, the Basilica is known for the tourists and especially the rooftop view of the Saint Marks Square.

Data

  • Location: Venice, Italy
  • Built: 11th century
  • Architectural Style: Italo-Byzantine
  • Dedicated To: Saint Mark the Evangelist
  • Famous Feature: Golden mosaics
  • Part of: St. Mark’s Square
