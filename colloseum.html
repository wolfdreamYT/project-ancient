The Colosseum

Overview

The Colosseum (Flavian Amphitheatre) is an iconic ancient Roman amphitheatre in the centre of Rome. it was completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus and hosted gladiator fights and public events for tens of thousands of people.

Key facts

  • Construction: Completed 80 AD under Emperor Titus
  • Capacity: Estimated 50,000–80,000 people
  • Uses: Games, public events, announcements
