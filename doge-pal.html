Doge’s Palace

Published on April 12, 2026

The Doge’s palace is located in Venice and is a historical building built by the Doge, who was the supreme authority of the former Venetian Republic, and was also used by the government.

Inside the Doge's Palace contains masterpieces from the gothic era, and is known for its arches, pink and white marble, and more. It also connected to a prison across the bridge of Sighs, which housed thousands of prisoners in the time.

The Doge's Palace is next to the Saint Marks Basilica, which was also an important component of venice and its people. Today, the Doge's Palace is visited daily and gives people a glimpse of what political power looked like in the Venetian Republic.

Data

  • Location: Venice, Italy
  • Built: 1340 (expanded over centuries)
  • Architectural Style: Venetian Gothic
  • Original Purpose: Government palace of the Venetian Republic
  • Connected Structure: Bridge of Sighs (to prison)
  • Current Use: Museum

  • Daily Visitors: ~7,000
