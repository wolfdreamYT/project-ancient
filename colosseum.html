The Colosseum

Published on March 31, 2026

The Colosseum was built around 80 CE, and was made for gladiator fights, public events, and executions. It was big enough to hold up to 80,000 spectators, and had an average capacity of 65,000 spectators. It had advanced architecture and technology, with trap doors, levers, and more.

The Colosseum is roughly 48 meters (157 feet) and is made of limestone, tuff (which is volcanic rock), and roman concrete (opus caementicium).

The colosseum had 80 verticle shafts and trap doors which allowed dramatic entrances of animals and gladiators. The architecture was strong enough to withstand earthquakes and thousands of years which is why it is so famous, gaining millions of tourists every year.
